Welcome to my homepage

I am an Astrophysics PhD student in the School of Mathematics, Statistics and Physics at Newcastle University, UK. My research focuses on the spectroscopy of massive stars, in particular understanding the connection between spectroscopic line profile variability and asteroseismology. Through this, I aim to probe the internal structure and dynamics of massive stars, including their rotation, mixing processes, and evolutionary pathways. I am also interested in binary stars and stellar multiplicity, and how combining spectroscopic diagnostics with asteroseismology can provide deeper insight into the physics governing massive star evolution.

As part of the SYMPHONY project at Newcastle University, I work under the supervision of Dr. Dominic Bowman on the spectroscopic modelling of line profile variability and macroturbulence, and their physical interpretation in terms of stellar pulsations and turbulent subsurface convection. Alongside my research, I am passionate about scientific computing and software development, both for academic and industry-oriented applications. Feel free to explore my research and code pages to learn more.
